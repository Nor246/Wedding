Dress code

A garden palette

We gather in a garden above the gorge, in sunset and candlelight. Please dress in a calm, natural palette — and if you add a touch of bordeaux, we'll be delighted. It sets the mood of the evening, though it's never required.

Bordeaux · accent

Build your look from the warm, pastel tones on the left. Bordeaux is our little wish — an optional accent, if you'd like to add it.

For her

A dress or suit in a neutral tone. Any cut — midi, maxi, or a jumpsuit.

  • A bordeaux accent is welcome — earrings, a clutch, a slim belt, or a ribbon in your hair.
  • A light shawl or wrap in bordeaux is lovely for the evening chill.
  • Shoes without a thin stiletto — there's grass and gravel underfoot.

Best avoided: white and ivory (the bride's colours), neon, sporty casual.

For him

A light-toned suit — beige, sand, or grey-olive. A cream or white shirt.

  • A bordeaux accent is welcome — a tie, bow tie, or pocket square.
  • The jacket can come off for dancing — but do bring it; the evening turns cool.

Best avoided: an all-black suit (too formal for a garden), shorts, sneakers, jeans.

Good to know

The ceremony and reception are outdoors, on grass and gravel paths. After sunset the mountains turn cool — bring a light extra layer.

Steady shoes are your friend — nothing with a sharp heel.
